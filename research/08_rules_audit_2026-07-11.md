## CHANGED (action needed)

*(These are places where our written assumptions differ from the current official text. Since the PDF is still v4, these are most likely errors in our docs rather than rule changes — either way the docs need fixing.)*

### C1. Contestants are NOT quarantined / devices are NOT surrendered the night before
- **Our assumption** (01_competition_intel.md, "Rules that can get you disqualified"): "phones/devices surrendered the night before each contest day."
- **Current official text:** "There is no quarantine for the contestants before the contest. This means that they can use their electronic devices before the contest." (Quarantine applies only to TLs/observers in the translation session: "all TLs and observers who are present in the translation session are under quarantine.")
- **Source:** rules PDF v4, §2.4 (translation/quarantine).
- **Impact:** Positive — you keep your phone/laptop the evening and morning before each contest day. You can do last-minute review of your at-home task notes and Gemma prompt bank right up to entering the hall. Devices still cannot be brought INTO the contest hall ("Prohibited Items: … electronic devices (computers, phones, earphones, calculators…)").

### C2. During the contest you see MORE than "only your own score"
- **Our assumption** (01_competition_intel.md): "You can typically only see your own score during the contest."
- **Current official text:** "during the contest, each contestant will have access only to their own scores in Scoreboard A (based on validation dataset) on each task. They cannot see the s[cores of others]… But they can see the Min_Score (score of the baseline solution) and the Max_Submission (the highest unnormalized score achieved by any other contestant at each time of the contest)." Also: "the feedback (submission score and the anonymous highest score achieved by any other contestant) is provided as the feedback to the contestant."
- **Source:** rules PDF v4, §2.6 Feedback (Scoreboard) and §2.5 dataset definitions.
- **Impact:** Actionable — you can always see the baseline score AND the current best anonymous submission per task. Use Max_Submission to decide where your time buys the most normalized points (if the top score on a task is barely above baseline, that task is where a small improvement is worth a lot on the GAITE-normalized scale).

## NEW (rules we never captured)

### N1. Official scores come from a hidden TEST set (Scoreboard B), not the live leaderboard
- **Official text:** "When the contestant submits a solution during the contest, the solution is scored based on the validation dataset… The results of evaluation, based on the 'Validation Dataset' are called the 'Scoreboard A'." / "When the contest is finished, the final solution of the contestant is evaluated based on the test dataset, and the results are called the 'Scoreboard B'. These results are used as the official scores of the contest: Official rankings, medals and awards are only based on the Scoreboard B scores."
- **Source:** rules PDF v4, §2.5–2.6.
- **Impact:** Big for a chatbot-driven contestant — the live score you see all day is NOT the score that decides your award. Don't spend hours squeezing tiny validation-leaderboard gains (overfitting the validation set can make Scoreboard B WORSE). Prefer simple, robust improvements; ask Gemma "how do I avoid overfitting to the validation set?" as a standard check.

### N2. Evaluation limits: 20-minute notebook runtime, max 60 submissions per task
- **Official text:** "For each task, the notebook runtime would be limited to a maximum 20 minutes, unless explicitly stated in the task statement." / "The maximum number of submissions per task would be 60." / "The contestants can make concurrent submissions, in that case, their submissions will be added to a queue and the evaluation results will be shown to them as soon as ready."
- **Source:** rules PDF v4, Technical Appendix §6 Evaluation Limits.
- **Impact:** Your submitted notebook must finish in ~20 min — no giant training runs inside the graded notebook. Plan with Gemma for "train small / fast" solutions. 60 submissions/task = ~10/hour is generous; submit early and often. Concurrent submissions mean you can queue one while working on the next idea.

### N3. Clarification questions must be Yes/No
- **Official text:** standard replies include "Please ask your question in Yes/No format" and "Please refer to the dataset and the baseline first when the task description is unclear"; "The Scientific Committee may deny responding to a ques[tion]".
- **Source:** rules PDF v4, §2.x Questions/Clarifications.
- **Impact:** If a statement confuses you, first paste it to Gemma for interpretation, and only file a clarification phrased as a Yes/No question.

### N4. Exact contest-day timing + obligatory practice round (schedule page)
- **Official text (schedule page):** "Practice Round – obligatory 07:30–09:30" (Aug 3); "Individua/GAITE Contest 1 … 09:00–15:00" (Aug 4); "Individua/GAITE Contest 2 … 09:00–15:00" (Aug 6); "GAITE Meeting / Alumni Meeting 14:00–15:00" (Aug 3).
- **Source:** https://ioai-official.org/republic-of-kazakhstan/schedule/
- **Impact:** Contests run 09:00–15:00 local (6h confirmed). The Aug 3 practice round is obligatory — that's your one chance to test the Gemma chat UI, JupyterLab, and to request a personal keyboard/mouse (see N6). There's also a dedicated GAITE Meeting Aug 3.

### N5. Team Challenge is two-stage in 2026 (Round 1 "Simulation" + top-10 Final)
- **Official text (schedule page):** "Team Challenge Round 1 (Simulation) 14:00–19:00" (Aug 3); "Team Challenge Final Round 09:00–12:00" at Alem.ai with "Transfer of top 10 teams" (Aug 7).
- **Source:** schedule page (above). PDF only says Team Challenge scoring "is task-specific and will be announced in the task statements."
- **Impact:** Team Challenge Round 1 happens on arrival+1 day (Aug 3 afternoon), right after the obligatory practice round — the first competition day is busier than our docs imply.

### N6. Own keyboard/mouse possible; no external monitor
- **Official text:** "Contestants may ask the Scientific Committee to bring their own keyboard or mouse to the contest hall. This request should be made during the practice session and may or may not be satisfied. Bringing an external monitor is not allowed."
- **Source:** rules PDF v4, §2.7 Supplies.
- **Impact:** If you type faster on your own keyboard, request it at the Aug 3 practice session.

### N7. GitHub Home-Task-3 changed on Jul 9 (after our docs were written)
- **Official commits** (github.com/IOAI-official/IOAI-2026, `Home Task/Home-Task-3.ipynb`):
  - 2026-07-09 `26591c2d` "Home-Task-3: batched fp16 predict_answers helper; reference precompute now minutes not 1.5h"
  - 2026-07-09 `379dc19a` "Home-Task-3: batching is a hint (tip only), not a provided helper — contestants optimize it themselves"
  - 2026-07-09 `4d02a6df` "Home-Task-3: fp16 fix for everyone (bf16 is slow on T4); batching stays a hint, not provided"
- **Source:** https://api.github.com/repos/IOAI-official/IOAI-2026/commits?since=2026-07-01 (saved to raw/gh_commits.json).
- **Impact:** Not a rules change, but if you already downloaded Home-Task-3, **re-download the notebook** — the reference code now uses fp16 (much faster) and batching is explicitly a contestant-side optimization hint. Since Day 1 tasks connect to at-home tasks, working from the stale notebook wastes prep time.

## CONFIRMED UNCHANGED (assumptions we rely on — all verified in the current v4 PDF)

1. **GAITE = Gemma 4, 2000 output tokens/query; Individual = Gemma 3, 1000 tokens.** Quote: "Individual Contest: Gemma 3, at most 1000 output tokens per query. ● GAITE Contest: Gemma 4, at most 2000 output tokens per query." (PDF, Tech Appendix §3 Language Models and AI Assistance.) External LLMs banned: "The use of any external LLMs, copilots, browser assistants, AI coding agents, or external APIs (including LLM APIs) is prohibited unless explic[itly allowed]".
2. **GAITE hints + same tasks/days/length.** Quote: "The tasks of GAITE Contest Day 1 and Day 2 are the same as the tasks of Individual Contest Day 1 and Day 2. The difference is that GAITE contestants are provided with hints and possibly more LLM support." and GAITE runs "two days with the same time and the same length as the Individual Contests 1 and 2." (PDF §4 GAITE Contest.)
3. **Separate GAITE scoreboard + GAITE-normalized scoring.** Quote: "There is a separate scoreboard for the GAITE Contest. It means the top score used for computing the normalized scores of the GAITE contest is the one achieved in the GAITE contest." (PDF §4.)
4. **Day 1 tasks connect to the at-home tasks; 6-hour days.** Quote: "each one of them is connected, in subject matter or method, to at least one of the at-home contest tasks. Contestants will have six hours to solve these problems." (PDF §2.1.) Day 2: "not necessarily connected to the At-Home tasks. Contestants have six hours". Schedule page corroborates 09:00–15:00.
5. **Python 3.13, pinned versions.** Quote: "The contest environment will use a pinned version of Python 3.13. Exact package versions will be published before the contest and fixed for all contestants." (Tech Appendix §1.)

Data processing: numpy, pandas, scipy, polars, pyarrow, h5py. Computer vision: opencv-python, Pillow, scikit-image, albumentations. Visualization: matplotlib, seaborn, plotly. Utilities/training: tqdm, joblib, tensorboard, pytorch-lightning, pydantic, pyyaml. … TensorFlow and Keras will not be available. 8. **Only pre-cached organizer models.** Quote: "Approved pretrained models will be pre-cached within the contest environment. Contestants may only use models explicitly provided by the organizers. External model downloads will not be permitted during the contest." (Tech Appendix §4.)
9. **Docs-site whitelist incl. huggingface.co/docs.** Quote: "The whitelist is expected to include: ● python.org ● pypi.org ● numpy.org ● pandas.pydata.org ● scikit-learn.org ● pytorch.org ● matplotlib.org ● scipy.org ● huggingface.co/docs Documentation websites for officially provid[ed tools]…" and "Network access outside the approved whitelist is prohibited." ## COULD NOT VERIFY

### U1. "Unlimited new chats" (playbook cornerstone) — NOT an official rule
- **Our assumption** (03_gemma4_playbook.md relies on it heavily; 01 doc already caveats "per current info"): "Unlimited new chats = unlimited retries."
- **Official text only says:** "The exact model, context window, rate limits, and usage quotas will be announced before the contest." (PDF Tech Appendix §3.) The word "unlimited" (and any chat-count rule) appears **nowhere** in the PDF or any fetched page — searched all extracted texts for "unlimited", "new chat", "conversation": zero hits.
- **Impact:** The fresh-chat-per-subproblem strategy is still sensible, but do NOT assume infinite quota. Add a contest-day step: at the obligatory Aug 3 practice round, confirm the actual rate limits/quotas, and have a fallback style (fewer, denser chats) in case quotas are tight.

### U2. "Make sure your best 2 submissions are selected" (playbook §7)
- No rule about selecting 2 submissions exists in the PDF. The PDF says "the final solution of the contestant is evaluated based on the test dataset" (§2.5) without spelling out how the "final solution" is designated (last submission? explicit selection?).
- **Impact:** Ask at the practice session how the final solution is designated; until then, treat "my latest submission = my official one" as the safe default and never leave a broken submission as your last.

### U3. Which Gemma 4 variant IOAI deploys / Gemma 4 family details
- Playbook's model-family claims (E2B, E4B, 26B-A4B, 31B, 12B-Unified, release window) come from third-party knowledge, not the official rules; the PDF explicitly defers: "The exact model… will be announced before the contest." No official page names a variant.

### U4. What exactly "changed recently" on the site
- The rules PDF is byte-identical in intent to what our docs describe: still Version 4, URL unchanged, `Last-Modified: 17 Jun 2026`. Probes for a v5/July PDF returned 404; the sitemap shows no new rules pages; Wayback has no 2026 snapshots of the rules page to diff (empty CDX result). The only post-July-1 official changes found anywhere are the three **Home-Task-3 notebook commits of Jul 9** (N7) and possibly schedule-page detail (N4/N5, which we had never captured, so cannot dated-diff). If the user saw a specific changed sentence, we could not reproduce it — the change they noticed may have been the GitHub task update.
